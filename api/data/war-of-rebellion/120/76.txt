FORT MONROE, VA., April 20, 1864.

Lieutenant-General GRANT, Washington, D. C.:

Instructions in regard to exchange of prisoners received and will be implicitly followed. I assume, however, that they are not intended to interfere with the special exchanges of sick and wounded prisoners on one side and the other now going on.

BENJ. F. BUTLER,

Major-General, Commanding.

WAR DEPARTMENT,

Washington, D. C., April 20, 1864-9. 30 p. m.

Major-General BUTLER, Fort Monroe, Va.:

Receive all the sick and wounded the Confederate authorities will send you, but send no more in exchange.

U. S. GRANT,

Lieutenant-General.

RICHMOND, VA., April 20, 1864.

General L. POLK, Demopolis, Ala.:

Send promptly to Andersonville, Ga., the Yankee prisoners at Cahaba and all others that may be captured. The present guard at Cahaba must go with the prisoners now sent and will remain at Andersonville.

S. COOPER,

Adjutant and Inspector General.

In the name of the laboring and landless
people of this country I insist that the public
lands shall, except in special cases, and for the
most urgent reasons, be reserved for homestead
entries. Our public domain is rapidly being
transferred into the hands of the few to the
prejudice of the many. And I shall insist, in
every proper way, that wherever lands shall be
granted to railroad companies to aid in the
construction of roads, that the companies shall
be required by law to sell the lands to actual
settlers at a price not exceeding $1 25 an acre.
This should in all oases be required by law.

gress ot the power to control the disposition of said

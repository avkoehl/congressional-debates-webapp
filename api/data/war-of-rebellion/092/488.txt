SPECIAL ORDERS, HDQRS. SEVENTEENTH ARMY CORPS, Numbers 285. In the Field, GA., November 18, 1864.

I. The orders for the movement to- morrow are as follows:

1. Brigadier General M. D. Leggett, commanding Third Division, will have the advance, and will move at 7 o'clock, on the road to Monticello.

2. Major General J. A. Mower, commanding First Division, will follow the Third Division, moving at 8 o'clock.

3. Brigadier General G. A. Smith, commanding Fourth Division, will follow the First Division, moving at 9 o'clock.

4. Major A. C. Waterhouse, commanding Artillery Brigade, will assign a battery to each division, to move as the division commander may direct.

5. The train of these headquarters will follow that portion of the ordnance train which moves in rear of the advance brigade of the Third Division.

II. Each division commander int his corps will at once cause to be detailed from his command ten good men who have had experience as teamsters, for driving in pontoon train, to report forthwith to Captain Buzard in charge.

By command of major General F. P. Blair:

C. CADLE, Jr.,Assistant Adjutant-General.

Mr. MORTON. I present to the Senate a
concurrent resolution of the Legislature of the
State of Georgia, assenting to certain condi-
tions in regard to the constitution of the State
of Georgia, imposed by Congress by the act of
1868. 1 ask to have the resolution read.
The Secretary read the resolution, as fol-
lows:
Whereas the Congress of the United States, by the
act of June 25,1868, proposing to admit the State of
Georgia to representation in Congress as a State of
the United States, imposes as one of the conditions
upon which the Same shall take effect, that the first
and third subdivisions of section seventeen of the
fifth article of the constitution of the State of Geor-
gia, except the proviso to the first subdivision, shall
be null and void, find that the .General Assembly
shall, by solemn act, declare the assent of the State
to the said condition: It is therefore
liesolved, by the House of Hepretentatives, (the Sen-
ate concurring,) That the General Assembly of the
State of Georgia does hereby declare the assent of
the State of Georgia to the said condition.

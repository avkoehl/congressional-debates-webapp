
SPECIAL FIELD ORDERS, HDQRS. DEPT. OF THE CUMBERLAND, Numbers 58. Nashville, Tenn., March 5, 1865.

* * * *

XII. The Eleventh Michigan Cavalry is hereby transferred from the command of Major-General Stoneman to the Sixth Cavalry Division. The regiment will, however, remain with General Stoneman until his expedition, when he will send it to Chattanooga, whence the commanding officer will report by telegraph to these headquarters for orders.

* * * *

By command of Major-General Thomas:

SOUTHARD HOFFMAN,

Assistant Adjutant-General.

[49.]

SPECIAL FIELD ORDERS, HDQS. DEPT. OF THE CUMBERLAND, Numbers 59. Nashville, Tenn., March 6, 1865.

* * * *

IV. The One hundred and forty-eighth Indiana Infantry will proceed to Columbia, Tenn., to relieve the Forty-seventh Missouri Volunteers at that place, reporting by telegraphupon its arrival to Brigadier General R. W. Johnson, at Pulaski, Tenn., The quartermaster's department will furnish the necessary transportation.

* * * *

By command of Major-General Thomas:

HENRY M. CIST,

Assistant Adjutant-General.

[49.]

SPECIAL FIELD ORDERS, HDQRS. DEPT. OF THE CUMBERLAND, Numbers 77. Nashville, March 24, 1865.

* * * *

XVIII. Brigadier General L. D. Watkins, U. S. Volunteers, will report to Major General John M. Palmer, commanding Department of Kentucky, for assignment to duty.

By command of Major-General Thomas:

SOUTHARD HOFFMAN,

Assistant Adjutant-General.

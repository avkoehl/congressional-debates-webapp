
CHAFFIN'S BLUFF, June 10, 1864.

General BRAGG:

The lookout near Cox's barn reports a large force of enemy moving in line of battle toward Howlett's house.

J. M. MAURY,

Lieutenant-Colonel, Commanding.
SPECIAL ORDERS, HDQRS. DEPT. OF N. C. AND S. VA., Numbers 21. June 10, 1864.

* * * * * * *

IV. Gracie's brigade will march at once and report to Major General B. R. Johnson to relieve Page's brigade in the trenches. The brigade will march by the shortest and quickest route. General Gracie will report sufficiently in advance of his brigade to receive orders from General Johnson as to its location.

By command of General Beauregard:

JNO. M. OTEY,Assistant Adjutant-General.

SPECIAL ORDERS, ADJT. AND INSP. GENERAL'S OFFICE, Numbers 135. Richmond, June 10, 1864.

* * * * * * *

XV. Colonel R. E. Withers, in addition to his duties as commandant post, Danville, Va., will report to Brigadier General J. L. Kemper, commanding reserve forces of Virginia, for the purpose of organizing, instructing, and commanding the reserves at Danville, Va.

* * * * * * *

XVII. Brigadier General W. M. Gardner, Provisional Army, C. S., will report to Major General Robert Ransom, jr., commanding Department of Richmond, for assignment to duty.

By command of the Secretary of War:

JNO. WITHERS,

Assistant Adjutant-General.

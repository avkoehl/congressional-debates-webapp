relief of Joseph Ormsby.

read a third time.

ing a pension to Joseph Ormsby, at the rate
of eightdollars per month, from June 19, 1862,
the date of the death of his son, Edward J.

application for under a misapprehension of
the law.

mendation that it do not pass, a bill (S. No.
866) for the relief of Mary F. Prindle.
Mr. POLAND. I desire to ask the House

The bill was read. It authorizes and directs
the Secretary of the Interior to pay to Mary
F. Prindle, widow of Jerome V. Prindle, late
a private in company H, first regiment Ver-
mont volunteers, from November 3, 1861, the
date of her husband's death, to November 10,
1868, the date of the commencement of her
present pension.
Mr. BENTON. I yield to the gentleman
from Vermont [Mr. Poland] for three min-
utes.
Mr. POLAND. Mr. Speaker, this bill merely
gives arrears of pension to Mrs. Prindle. Her
application was made in due season, within the
five years allowed by law ; but the lawyer who
was employed by her to make application for
this pension was taken sick and, after being
sick for a year or two, finally died. Alter his
death the papers relating to^ the application
were found laid away in a pigeon hole in his
office. 1 have understood from the chairman of
the Committee on Pensions, [Mr. BiiNJAMlK,]
who is now absent, that the commitSee have
reported bills of precisely the same character,
which have been passed by the House and sent
to the Senate. This is a bill passed by the Sen-
ate and referred in this Honseto our committee.
I learned from the chairman of the committee
that they were holding on to this ease in the
committee as a sort of lever to compel the
Senate to pass House bills of precisely similar
character which have been sent to the Senate.
There was no objection on the part of the
chairman of the committee to the passage of
this bill, as upon evidence of the same charac-
ter similar bills have been reported by the
committee, passed by this House, and are now
pending in the Senate.
The bill was ordered to a third reading; and
it was accordingly read the third time, and
passed.

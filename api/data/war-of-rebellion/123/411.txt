ADJUTANT-GENERAL'S OFFICE,

Washington, D. C., August 19, 1862-1 p. m.

Lieutenant CHARLES S. BOWMAN,

Disbursing Officer, Fort Leavenworth, Kans.:

Recruits for negro regiments will under no circumstance be paid bounty and premium.

By order of the Secretary of War:

THOMAS M. VINCENT,

Assistant Adjutant-General.,

ADJUTANT-GENERAL'S OFFICE,

Washington, D. C., August 19, 1862.

His Excellency the GOVERNOR OF KANSAS,

Atchison, Kans.:

Senator Lane, as commissioner of recruiting, has authority to appoint second lieutenants, under Order Numbers 75. If you have made any such appointments, please recall them. They cannot be recognized.

By order:

T. M. VINCENT,

Assistant Adjutant-General.

WAR DEPARTMENT,

Washington City, D. C., August 19, 1862.

J. W. FINNELL,

Frankfort, Ky.:

Cavalry arms and equipments were sent and have, no doubt, been received by General Boyle, the proper officer at Louisville., Ky. Under what authority are you acting? There seems to be a considerable number of persons acting in Kentucky independent of each other.

EDWIN M. STANTON,

Secretary of War.

FRANKFORT, KY., August 19, 1862.

(Received 8.20 p. m.)

Honorable E. M. STANTON:

I applied to General Boyle, the proper officer at Louisville, for arms, and the dispatch sent yesterday was after application to him. I am acting under the authority of the Governor of Kentucky, and in conjunction with the Military Board, and of obedience to orders of the Secretary of War. I am, according to the laws of Kentucky, the proper officer in the State to superintend recruiting, and have performed that service since September last.

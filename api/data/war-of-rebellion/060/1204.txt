WAR DEPARTMENT, C. S. A., Richmond, Va., March 1, 1864.

COMMANDER AT PETERSBURG, VA.:

The enemy are said to have crossed a small raiding force across the James, about 17 miles west of this city, with the view of striking the railroads on the south side. I consider this doubtful. Skirmishing on the east side of the city.

J. A. SEDDON,

Secretary of War.

ORANGE COURT-HOUSE, March 2, 1864.

General S. COOPER:

All quiet in front. Keep me advised of movements of Kilpatrick.

R. E. LEE.

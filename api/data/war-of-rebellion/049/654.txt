ADJUTANT AND INSPECTOR GENERAL'S OFFICE,

September 2, 1863.

Respectfully referred to Chief of Ordnance.

By command of Secretary of War:

H. L. CLAY,

Assistant Adjutant- General.

SEPTEMBER 3, 1863.

Unless these torpedoes can be continually repoaced as exploded, I doubt the poicy of using them at all. To use them once only is to irritate, not intimadatre. If, therefore, we have no sufficient commad of the vicinity to continue the use of these impediments, it seems to me impolitic to begin their use. They can be furnished here whenever called for.

J. GORGAS.

SEPTEMBER 3, 1863.

ORDNANCE BUREAU:

Have yu an officer or employe you can send to use them!

J. A. S. [SEDDON].

SECRETARY.

Aman can be sent.

J. GORGAS.

SEPTEMBER 9, 1863.

ADJUTANT- GENERAL:

Revoke Castlemen's authority.

J. A. S. [SEDDON],

Secretary.

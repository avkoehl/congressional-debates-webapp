Congressional Debates

This website provides an API to several Natural Language Processing methods implemented on the Congressional Globe corpus (https://memory.loc.gov). The Congressional Globe contains the recorded transcription of the proceedings of each session of Congress from the 23rd to the 42nd Congress, covering the time period 1833 to 1873. I am most interested in models that attempt to track chronologically when the meaning of a word has significantly changed. The first tab, "Frequency" simply allows for you to search for any word to follow the change in its relative usage throughout the congressional debates. The second tab, "Distributional Models", interfaces with my implementation of different models for tracking linguisitic word shift that have been presented recently and seem to hold lots of potential. Please keep in mind that the results are experimental and are meant more to provide a first glance of this complex corpus, and hopefully, a place to start looking.

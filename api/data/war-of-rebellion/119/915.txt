
WASHINGTON, February 4, 1864.

To the SENATE:

In complicate with the resolution of the Senate of the 26th ultimo, requesting "a copy of all the correspondence between the authorities of the United States and the rebel authorities on the exchange of prisoners and the different propositions connected with that subject," I transmit here with a report from the Secretary of War and the papers with which it is accompanied.

ABRAHAM LINCOLN.

[Inclosures.]

WAR DEPARTMENT, Washington City, February 4, 1864.

The PRESIDENT:

SIR: I have the honor to return herewith the resolution of the Senate of the 26th ultimo, requesting "a copy of all the correspondence between the authorities of the United States and the rebel authorities on the exchange of prisoners and the different propositions connected with that subject," and to transmit a copy of the report of Major-General Hitchcock, commissioner for exchanges, with the accompanying papers.

Very respectfully, your obedient servant,

EDWIN M. STANTON,

Secretary of War.

WASHINGTON, D. C., February 4, 1864.

SIR: I have the honor to inclose a copy of "the correspondence between the authorities of the United States and the rebel authorities on the exchange of prisoners and the different propositions connected with that subject," called for by the Senate of the United States by resolution of the 26th ultimo. *


MAJOR-GENERAL STEEDMAN'S HEADQUARTERS, December 10, 1864.

Major General G. H. THOMAS:

Your message received. The enemy's lines and position appear to be unchanged. He is perfectly quiet. I am confident his right has not been changed since I saw you.

JAMES B. STEEDMAN,

Major-General of Volunteers.

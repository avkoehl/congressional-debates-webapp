Mr. COBB. I could not ask the House to do

Government is directly interested. It is a small
matter, and the committee have unanimously in-

according to the legal manner of surveying lands,
but had to be cut up into small fractions. The De-

be regarded as valid, in order that the Govern-

ter. Every member of the committee who was
present agreed that the bill should be reported. It
was reported by me at the last session, and passed
the House without a dissenting voice, ana went
to the Senate; but failed there for want of time.
It is a matter in which the Government is inter-
ested. The island cannot be surveyed in any
other way than the one in which it has been sur-
veyed, and all the committee ask is that the sur-
vey shall be approved. Now, will the House
allow the bill to pass ?

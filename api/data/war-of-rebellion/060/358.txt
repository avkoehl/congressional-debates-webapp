PONY MOUNTAIN, January 6, 1864-11 a. m.

Captain NORTON:

Can discover no alteration from previous reports of the position across the river. In the direction of Manassas and Thornton Gaps atmosphere too smoky for observation. Have seen no signal flag as yet on Cedar Mountain.

PAINE.

CULPEPER, VA., January 6, 1864.

Captain E. B. PARSONS,

Acting Assistant Adjutant-General:

All quiet on the line of pickets. I have made every possible inquiry in regard to Starke's Ford. Had before I inquired of headquarters, but can find out nothing. I will send the force as directed.

W. MERRITT,

Brigadier-General.

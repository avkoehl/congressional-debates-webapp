
CUMBERLAND, January 8, 1864.

Brigadier-General AVERELL,

Martinsburg:

To put a stop to further controversy at New Creek concerning questions of jurisdiction, I issue the following instructions to-day to the commanding officer First Virginia Cavalry: "You will promptly obey all orders from Colonel Mulligan, commanding division, during the continuance of the present emergency. "

B. F. KELLEY,

Brigadier-General.

HEADQUARTERS ARMY OF THE POTOMAC,

January 9, 1864-9 a. m.

Major General JOHN SEDGWICK,

Headquarters Sixth Corps:

I have the honor to inform you that Major-General Meade left for Washington this morning, to be absent a few days, and that the command of this army during his absence devolves upon you.

S. WILLIAMS,

Assistant Adjutant-General.

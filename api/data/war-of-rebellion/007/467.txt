HEADQUARTERS DEPARTMENT OF THE OHIO,Louisville, Ky., December 2, 1861.

Colonel CURRAN POPE,

Commanding Fifteenth Regiment, Kentucky Volunteers:

SIR: The general commanding has placed upon you the responsibility of guarding against all injury, whether through neglect or design, the railroad and turnpike brigade at and near New Haven, and also the railroad bridge over the Bushy Fork of Salt River and the turnpike bridge over Rolling Fork in advance of New Hanen.*

JAMES B. FRY,

Assistant Adjutant-General.
HEADQUARTERS DEPARTMENT OF THE OHIO,Louisville, Ky., December 2, 1861.

Colonel WANTER,

Commanding Regiment Kentucky Volunteers:

SIR: The general has placed upon you the responsibility of guarding the line of railroad from Lexington to Covington.

The headquarters of your regiment will be established at Falmounth, and you will post your companies so as to effectually guard all the important brigade on the line.*

Very respectfully,

JAMES B. FRY,

Assistant Adjutant-General.

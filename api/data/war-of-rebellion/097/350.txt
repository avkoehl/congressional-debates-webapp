HEADQUARTERS ARMY OF THE POTOMAC,March 31, 1865-3.45 p. m.

Major-General HUMPHREYS,

Commanding Second Corps:

Major-General Warren is on the White Oak road, and states that he has moved his whole corps up except one brigade, and will hold it. Captured flag from Fifty-sixth Virginia, Hunton's brigade. Sent you for your information.

A. S. WEBB,

Brevet Major-General and Chief of Staff.
HEADQUARTERS ARMY OF THE POTOMAC,March 31, 1865-5 p. m.

Major-General HUMPHREYS,

Commanding Second Corps:

Major-General Warren has been ordered to intrench himself in his present position on the White Oak road. You are to connect your left with his right to hold as much of the new line as possible to enable him to have the maximum reserves to protect his left flank. You will intrench your portion of the new line.

ALEX. S. WEBB,

Brevet Major-General and Chief of Staff.
HEADQUARTERS SECOND ARMY CORPS,March 31, 1865-5.25 p. m.

Brevet Major-General WEBB,

Chief of Staff:

Four officers and fifty-nine prisoners have been sent in from Miles' center-four from Hunton's brigade, Pickett's division, the rest from Wise's brigade. Other prisoners are coming in.

A. A. HUMPHREYS,

Major-General.

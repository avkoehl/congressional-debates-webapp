EXECUTIVE COMMUNICATIONS.
The PRESIDENT pro tempore laid before
the Senate a message from the President of
the United States, transmitting a report and
accompanying papers from the Secretary of
State, in compliance with the eighteenth sec-
tion of the act approved August 18, 1850, reg-
ulating the diplomatic and consular systems
of the United States; which was referred to
the Committee on Commerce, and ordered to
be printed.
He also laid before the Senate a report from '
the Secretary of State, communicating, in com-
pliance with a resolution of the Senate of the
19th ultimo, a statement of the amounts paid
for publishing the laws of the United States in
the newspapers appointed for that purpose
since the 4th of March, 1801; and also a state-
ment of the amount paid by that Department
for consul fees and reporting trials since March
4,1861; which was ordered to lie on the table,
and be printed.
He also laid before the Senate a message
from the President of the United States, trans-
mitting a report from the Secretary of the
Treasury, containing the information requested
in a resolution of the Senate of the 16th ultimo
relative to the amount of United States bonds
issued to the Union Pacific Railroad Company
and each of its branches, including the Central
Pacific railroad of California, and the amount
of interest repaid by the several railroad com-
panies to the United States; which was referred
to the Committee on the Pacific Railroad, and
ordered to be printed.

Mr. SHERMAN. I am directed by the Com-
mittee on Finance, to whom was referred the
bill (H. R. No. 3G9) to prevent frauds in the
collection of the tax on distilled spirits, to re-
port it back with an amendment; and I am
also directed, as the immediate consideration
of this bill will greatly advance the public
interest, to ask the consent of the Senate to
put it on its passage now.
There being no objection, the Senate, as in
Committee of the Whole, proceeded to consider
the bill, which provides that from and after its
passage no distilled spirits in bond shall be
withdrawn or removed from any warehouse for
the purpose of transportation, redistillation,
rectification, change of package, exportation,
or for any other purpose whatever, until ihe
full tax on such spirits shall have been duly
paid to the collector of the proper district.
The Committee on Finance reported the bill
with an amendment in line four, to strike out
the words "in bond;" so as to make the bill
read:
That from and after the passage of this net no dis-

warehouse for' the purpose oi transportation, redis-
